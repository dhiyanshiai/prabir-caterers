Unlike modern cities, Varanasi weddings often take place inside narrow *callis* (lanes), Katras, and heritage neighborhoods.

This creates challenges—and traditions—that outsiders rarely understand.

## Understanding the Katra Environment

Katras are:
*   **Narrow**
*   **Residential**
*   **Community-driven**

Yet, they have hosted weddings for centuries.

---

## Key Challenges

### 1. Limited Vehicle Access
No large trucks. No shortcuts. Everything must be carried by hand or small carts.

### 2. Crowd Sensitivity
Neighbors are part of the ecosystem. Noise and space need to be managed respectfully.

### 3. Space Constraints
Every square foot matters. Kitchens are often set up on rooftops or in tight courtyards.

---

## How Experienced Teams Manage This

*   **Modular equipment** designed for narrow passage.
*   **Manual transport planning** (Thela/Trolley logistics).
*   **Time-based loading schedules** (Late night/Early morning).
*   **Community coordination** to ensure smooth flow.

---

## Why Outsiders Struggle

Vendors unfamiliar with Banaras often:
1.  **Block lanes**, causing traffic jams.
2.  **Misjudge space**, leading to cramped setups.
3.  **Create friction with locals** due to lack of communication.

Local teams plan *with* the neighborhood, not against it.

Banarasi weddings succeed because:
